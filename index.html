“In most books, the I, or first person, is omitted; in this it will be retained; that, in respect to egotism, is the main difference. We commonly do not remember that it is, after all, always the first person that is speaking.

I should not talk so much about myself if there were anybody else whom I knew as well. Unfortunately, I am confined to this theme by the narrowness of my experience. Moreover, I, on my side, require of every writer, first or last, a simple and sincere account of his own life, and not merely what he has heard of other men's lives; some such account as he would send to his kindred from a distant land; for if he has lived sincerely, it must have been in a distant land to me.

Perhaps these pages are more particularly addressed to poor students. As for the rest of my readers, they will accept such portions as apply to them. I trust that none will stretch the seams in putting on the coat, for it may do good service to him whom it fits.”

― Henry David Thoreau, Walden

Basketball

It’s not just a game that includes a ball,

this is a game with a rise and a fall.

The players work hard and hardly ever rest,

their names printed proudly across their vest.

The ball bounces many of times,

and goes through the net as the buzzer chimes.

A slam dunk or alley-oop the skills are all there,

as the ball flies high up into the air.

Basketball is not just a game, it’s an art,

where the game is decided right from the start.

Basketball is all about the team,

as each individual has their own dream.

It’s not just about those points on the board,

but the hard work winning shot as the point is scored.

I love the gym; I love basketball. I'm really passionate about it.

---Kyrie Irving
